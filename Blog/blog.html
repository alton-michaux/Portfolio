Discovering My Passion

10/2020

Here I am, 34 years old at a job that was supposed to be temporary 12 years ago.

I've always had a goal of escaping at some point, but it was always easy to dash those dreams with a family to feed. In a lot of ways this factory has been my security blanket making it easier to ignore my own potential and settle for an existence that required less effort.

However, with the recent pandemic and the state of things not looking so good at work, I knew now was time to make a change.

I first learned about "Code the Dream" through a text from my mother. She's always sending me opportunities she thinks I'd be interested in, but something grabbed me instantly when I read her text with a link to a program called "Code the Dream".

After the initial application and a few hours of pre-work I realized how valuable a skillset this is and that it's something that I need to learn.

I know this might not seem like a big deal, but I've spent the last 12 years delusional and wasting time with things I knew were never going to take to me to any legitimate career, at least not from the role that I'm currently playing in the life of my family.

It feels really good to finally have direction!

5 Year Plans

10/10/2020

Coding isn't my first rodeo as far as learning new things: I've recently spent a few months learning to trade stocks and other financial markets and this summer I basically tore my lawnmower apart and put it back together with the help of some YouTube videos. Something felt different about coding tho, I've been modestly "successful" in my little learning adventures, but I constantly crave coding knowledge.

I had actually begun to put together a couple of "5-year plans" that would actually give me a legitimate chance of achieving my goals, but they were all vastly dependent on me alone. This isn't to say that I see that as a bad thing or that coding will be any different, it would just be really valuable to have mentors break this stuff down and get you to a point to where people are going to want to hire you based on your actual, provable skills as opposed to what college you graduated from or if you even went to college at all.

Well, that's where I just want to thank Khan Academy for providing this awesome free platform to keep learning and furthering myself every single day.

Even if I'm not accepted to CTD in a couple of weeks, I fully intend to learn and become proficient in multiple coding languages within a year.

The end goal is a job in web devolopment or software engineering.

Becoming a Code Samurai

After 17 days of anxious energy and bi-polar mood swings, I finally got my acceptance email! This is a huge deal guys! (for me at least)
I can finally move forward with a solid plan knowing that I'll be taking this path for the next 6 months (and beyond).

My plan is to be out of my current job situation and in the tech field within one year.

Let's do this.
